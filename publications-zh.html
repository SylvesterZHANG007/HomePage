Enhancing Grasping Diversity With a Pinch-Suction and Soft-Rigid Hybrid Multimodal Gripper

Y. Zhao, J. Zhu, J. Zhang, S. Zhang, M. Shao, Z. Chai, Y. Liu, J. Wu, Z. Wu, J. Zhang
IEEE Transactions on Robotics, Vol. 41, pp. 3890-3907
摘要 ▼

Multimodal grasping has emerged as an effective strategy to enhance grasping diversity for grippers in response to rapidly expanding application scenarios. Among various designs, pinch-suction hybrid mechanisms and soft-rigid hybrid structures have been demonstrated as two practical strategies for achieving multimodality. However, existing research on these two strategies still lacks simple and effective synergistic mechanisms to fully leverage the advantages of various modes while ensuring mutual non-interference. This paper proposes a pinch-suction and soft-rigid hybrid multimodal gripper (HMG) that integrates four operating modes in a compact structure. Two simple and effective synergistic mechanisms are introduced to coordinate pinch and suction operations and the cooperation between soft and rigid components, respectively. Through the collaboration of different modes, HMG exhibits competitive grasping diversity in four aspects, including weight (from 0.2g to 10kg), fragility (from jelly to aluminum profiles), size (from 0.46mm to 0.55m), and shape (from poorly pinchable to poorly suckable). We further demonstrate its adaptability and robustness in handling irregularly shaped objects, as well as its proficiency in executing complex real-world manipulation tasks, underwater operations, and closed-loop grasping. Its enhanced grasping diversity is expected to accelerate diverse applications in daily life, industrial environments, and underwater scenarios.

Synergizing Structural Stiffness Regulation with Compliance Contact Stiffness: Bio-Inspired Soft Stimuli-Responsive Materials Design for Soft Machines

K. Ma, J. Zhang, R. Sun, B. Chang, S. Zhang, X. Wang, J. Wu, J. Zhang
Advanced Engineering Materials, Vol. 26, Issue 10, pp. 2400461
摘要 ▼

Stiffness regulation strategies endow soft machines with enhanced functionality to address diverse application demands, such as increasing structural stiffness to manipulate heavy objects. However, most programmable stiffness strategies typically struggle to maintain inherent compliant interaction capabilities after structural stiffness enhancement. In this study, inspired by the musculoskeletal system, we propose a soft stimuli-responsive material (SRM) by integrating shape memory alloys into compliant materials. Through mechanical performance characterization, the bending modulus increases from 6.6 MPa to 142.4 MPa under active stimulation, spanning two orders of magnitude, while the Young's modulus remains at 2.2 MPa during structural stiffness programming. This comparative result indicates that our SRM can maintain low contact stiffness for compliant interaction while increasing structural stiffness. Subsequently, we develop three different soft machines to demonstrate the application potential of this smart material, such as robotic grippers, wearable devices, and deployable mechanisms. By applying our material, these machines possess enhanced load-bearing capabilities. Meanwhile, these demonstrations also illustrate the effectiveness of this paradigm in regulating the structural stiffness of soft machines while maintaining their compliant interaction capabilities.

Transporting Dispersed Cylindrical Granules: An Intelligent Strategy Inspired by an Elephant Trunk

Y. Zhao, J. Zhang, S. Zhang, P. Zhang, G. Dong, J. Wu, J. Zhang
Advanced Intelligent Systems, Vol. 5, Issue 10, pp. 2300182
摘要 ▼

Manipulating granular materials is a key function of robotic grippers. However, existing approaches are always inefficient when dealing with large amounts of dispersed granules. To overcome this challenge, we draw inspiration from African elephants (Loxodonta Africana), which can utilize finger-like extensions at the tip of their trunks to efficiently grasp dispersed granular food in one go by regulating the state transition of granules. This paper integrates this bioinspired intelligent strategy into a soft pneumatic gripper for transporting dispersed granules. To evaluate the critical driving pressure when grasping granules, experiments construct a library and examine the effects of initial relative height and relative lifting speed on grasping success rate. Experimental results demonstrate that this elephant trunk-inspired robotic strategy achieves a success rate exceeding 90% and saves approximately 50% operation time compared to individual grasping approaches. This study may provide new insights for novel manipulation strategies for efficiently transporting dispersed granular materials.

Spiral Genesis: Intelligent Self-Reproducing Robots Evolving from 2D Lattices to 3D DNA-Inspired Architectures

S. Zhang, H. Lipson
Target Journal: Science Robotics
摘要 ▼

Self-replication is a fundamental principle of biological systems, offering pathways for scalable and autonomous construction in engineering environments. Here, we propose a conceptual framework for modular self-reproducing robots designed to operate under extreme or inaccessible conditions where human intervention is limited or unfeasible. Inspired by cellular division, the system consists of identical modules capable of magnetic connection, disconnection, and reconfiguration through a hinge-aligned stacking mechanism. This architecture supports smooth, continuous 3D transformations and recursive structural reproduction with multiple degrees of freedom, including DNA-like configurations. The proposed design supports dynamic reassembly and structural scalability, laying the foundation for autonomous robotic systems capable of adaptive growth. Future work will focus on hardware prototyping, learning-based simulation in MuJoCo, structural complexity analysis, and sim-to-real transfer, aiming to systematically explore the feasibility of this bio-inspired strategy and establish theoretical foundations for future applications in space and other high-risk environments.

From Structural Design to Dynamics Modeling: Control-Oriented Development of a 3-RRR Parallel Ankle Rehabilitation Robot

S. Zhang, Y. Zhang, J. Lyu, S. K. Agrawal
Target Conference: IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation (ICRA)
arXiv Preprint: arXiv:2505.13762
摘要 ▼

This paper presents the development of a wearable ankle rehabilitation robot based on a 3-RRR spherical parallel mechanism (SPM), supporting multi-degree-of-freedom recovery through pitch, roll, and yaw motions. The system features a compact, ergonomic architecture designed for comfort, safety, and compatibility with ankle joint biomechanics. A complete design-to-dynamics pipeline has been implemented, including structural design, kinematic modeling for motion planning, and Lagrangian-based dynamic modeling for torque estimation and simulation analysis. Preliminary simulations validate stable joint coordination and smooth motion tracking under representative rehabilitation trajectories. Control frameworks are currently being developed to enhance responsiveness across the entire workspace. Future work will focus on integrating personalized modeling and adaptive strategies, addressing kinematic singularities through model-based control. This work establishes a foundational platform for intelligent, personalized ankle rehabilitation, supporting static training and potential gait phase-timed assistance extensions.

Self-Evolving Design Framework for Personalized Wearable Robots Based on Human Motion

S. Zhang, Z. Nian
Target Journal: Nature
摘要 ▼

The design of wearable robots has traditionally relied on fixed templates and manual engineering, limiting their adaptability to individual needs and accessibility in resource-constrained environments. Here, we introduce a self-evolving framework that generates personalized soft-rigid hybrid wearable robots directly from human motion. By capturing joint trajectories through commercial video and extracting task-specific kinematic subspaces, our system infers the minimal degrees of freedom required to reproduce individual movement. These constraints are embedded into a continuous design space that integrates multiple actuation modalities, including pneumatic, cable-driven, and phase-change elements. Using a differentiable optimization pipeline that combines finite element modeling with reinforcement learning, the framework co-optimizes structure, material distribution, and control to minimize energy consumption and mechanical complexity. Analysis of thousands of automatically generated morphologies reveals generalizable relationships linking joint complexity, material composition, and metabolic efficiency. Prototypes fabricated within 24 hours demonstrate functional performance across multiple joints. This approach enables data-driven generation of customized rehabilitation devices and suggests a broader paradigm of human-machine co-adaptation based on individual biomechanics.

Status: Manuscript in Preparation
